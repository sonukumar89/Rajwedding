One of the things that makes photography a successful art form is that it can show a viewer things in a different way. As photographers, we notice small details, weird juxtapositions and the small oddities of daily life. We use many tools to capture our subject matter in a different way: a variety of lenses, changing our camera angle, using long exposures, or very fast ones. But this tutorial is going to I'm going to try to dispel some common beliefs about details shots. I believe they can have entire people in them, be shot with a wide angle lens and that they don't always have to be taken with a $2,000 macro setup. So for the purposes of this tutorial I'd like to propose three broad aspects of detail shots in order to define the subject.focus on the technique of the detail shot. By getting up close and personal with our subjects, we can show our audience things they rarely experience.
